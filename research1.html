UF Cryogenic Heat Transfer Lab: Cryogenic Line Chilldown Experiment for Vertical Pipes

At this lab, we run experiments to simulate line chilldown process involved in Spaceflight Launch Countdowns. During Propellant loading for cryogenic fuels and oxidisers, the pipes from the propellant tanks in ground systems to the launch vehicle will have to be chilldown with intial flow of the propellant to bring the pipe material to same temperatures as that of propellant to avoid two-phase losses. We are working on reducing the losses incurred using Liquid Nitrogen and estimating the boiling curve. I am currently working on quenching front dynamics for 4ft long pipes for LN2 System Line chilldown and its depdencies with Reynolds number. This work is relevant towards my Masters Thesis.
 

 

 

 

 

Advanced System Integration Intern: Cummins Inc.

In Summer'2019, I interned with Cummins Inc. at Columbus, IN on Engine Optimization for SuperTruck2 program under DOE to produce high thermal efficiency diesel engines. I had the brief experience in working with the GT Power Software through which I had to correlate and integrate the results at system level (Close, Open and Mechanical Efficiencies), identify causes for loss or gain of effiencies from each sub-system, and validate the results with that of the experimental data from the Prototype Tests. As part of the training, I had attended several courses (Turbomachinery, Engine Performance, Engine Design, various in-house software) and hands-on engine B series engine training.
 

 

 

 

 

ISRO: Static testing & Experimental Aeroacoustics

At the Satish Dhawan Space Center, ISRO, located in Sriharikota, India which is the Launch complex for the Launch Vehicles namely, Geostationary Satellite Launch Vehicle (GSLV) Mk2 & Mk3 and, Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) where I worked as a Graduate Engineer Trainee with the Vehicle Assembly and Static Test Facility. Apart from Assisting in static tests, I worked on design of Jet deflectors at Launch pads at computational and experimental levels. This opportunity gave me a deep understanding of multiphase flows and acoustics involved at Launch of a rocket.

During my senior year, I interned with Indian Space Research Organization at the Satish Dhawan Space Center, India.I worked with the team in-charge for the propellant loading into the liquid stages of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and Geostationary Satellite Launch Vehicle (GSLV) Mk 2 & 3. I worked with programming ARM Processors ans writing control logic of temperature control. This experience gave me a closer insight on the working of liquid rocket engines.
